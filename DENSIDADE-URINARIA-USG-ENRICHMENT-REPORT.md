### 1. Central and Nephrogenic Diabetes Insipidus (2025)
- **Journal:** Frontiers in Endocrinology
- **Authors:** Flynn K, Hatfield J, Brown K
- **DOI:** 10.3389/fendo.2024.1479764
- **Type:** Review
- **Published:** January 15, 2025

**Key Findings:**
- Water deprivation test: healthy kidneys concentrate urine >800 mOsm/kg, DI patients maintain <300 mOsm/kg
- After desmopressin: central DI shows >50% osmolality increase, nephrogenic DI <15%
- Copeptin >21.4 pmol/L diagnoses nephrogenic DI with 100% sensitivity/specificity
- USG <1.008 indicates hyposthenuria characteristic of diabetes insipidus

### 2. Criterion Values for Hydration Assessment (2017)
- **Journal:** European Journal of Clinical Nutrition
- **Authors:** Perrier ET, Bottin JH, Vecchio M, Lemetais G
- **DOI:** 10.1038/ejcn.2016.269
- **Type:** Research Article
- **Published:** February 1, 2017

**Key Findings:**
- USG ≥1.013 identifies concentrated urine (osmolality >500 mOsm/kg) with 98.4% accuracy (AUC 0.984)
- Maintaining 24-hour urine osmolality ≤500 mOsm/kg is desirable for renal health
- Strong sensitivity and specificity as practical hydration monitoring tool
- 817 samples analyzed from 82 healthy French adults

### 3. Adult Dehydration (2025)
- **Journal:** StatPearls Publishing
- **Authors:** Taylor K, Tripathi AK
- **DOI:** NBK555956
- **Type:** Review
- **Published:** March 5, 2025

**Key Findings:**
- USG >1.020 and osmolality >450 mOsm/kg indicate concentrated urine
- Serum osmolality >295 mOsm/kg suggests dehydration
- 2015 Cochrane review: urinary biomarkers unreliable when used alone in elderly
- Clinical signs (skin turgor, dry mouth) should NOT be used alone for hydration assessment in elderly

### 4. Hydration Status in Athletes (2016)

- **DOI:** 10.1519/JSC.0000000000001298
- **Type:** Research Article
- **Published:** June 1, 2016

**Key Findings:**
- USG is sensitive and specific indicator for pre-exercise hydration

### Reference Ranges
- **Normal USG:** 1.003-1.030
- **Adequate Hydration:** 1.010-1.020
- **Concentrated (Dehydration):** ≥1.020
- **Dilute (Overhydration):** <1.010
- **Hyposthenuria (DI):** <1.008

### Diagnostic Thresholds
- **Concentrated Urine Detection:** USG ≥1.013 (98.4% accuracy)
- **Dehydration (Serum):** Osmolality >295 mOsm/kg
- **Dehydration (Urine):** USG >1.020 or osmolality >450 mOsm/kg
- **Target for Renal Health:** 24h osmolality ≤500 mOsm/kg

### Diabetes Insipidus Diagnosis
- **Water Deprivation Test:**
  - Normal: urine concentration >800 mOsm/kg
  - DI: <300 mOsm/kg
- **Desmopressin Response:**
  - Central DI: >50% increase
  - Nephrogenic DI: <15% increase
- **Copeptin:** >21.4 pmol/L = nephrogenic DI (100% sensitivity/specificity)

## Scientific Articles Added (4 new articles)

### Article 1: Estradiol Reference Intervals (LC-MS/MS)
- **Authors:** Verdonk SJE, Vesper HW, Martens F, Sluss PM, Hillebrand JJ, Heijboer AC
- **Journal:** Clinica Chimica Acta
- **Date:** 2019-08-01
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.cca.2019.04.062
- **Type:** Research Article
- **Summary:** Establishes harmonized estradiol reference intervals using high-precision LC-MS/MS method. Analyzes 30 premenopausal women with daily monitoring through complete menstrual cycles. Demonstrates cyclical variations with lower levels in early follicular phase and peaks during LH surge (275-2864 pmol/L). Method shows traceability to CDC reference standards.

### Article 2: Physiology, Menstrual Cycle
- **Authors:** Thiyagarajan DK, Basit H, Jeanmonod R
- **Journal:** StatPearls Publishing
- **Date:** 2024-09-27
- **PMID:** 29763196
- **Type:** Review
- **Summary:** Comprehensive review of menstrual cycle physiology from StatPearls. Covers hormonal regulation, cycle phases, and clinical significance. Describes in detail the early follicular phase, including basal estradiol levels (25-75 pg/mL on day 3), FSH-mediated follicular recruitment, and gradual estradiol production by developing follicles.

### Article 3: Proliferative and Follicular Phases
- **Authors:** Monis CN, Tetrokalashvili M
- **Journal:** StatPearls Publishing
- **Date:** 2022-09-12
- **PMID:** 31194386
- **Type:** Review
- **Summary:** Descriptive article about the follicular and proliferative phases of the female menstrual cycle. Explains the process of ovarian follicle maturation during the follicular phase, preparing them for ovulation. Addresses concomitant endometrial changes and clinical importance of early follicular phase for ovarian reserve and reproductive function assessment.

### Article 4: Fertility Indicator Using Estradiol
- **Authors:** Usala SJ, Trindade AA
- **Journal:** Medicina (Kaunas)
- **Date:** 2020-10-22
- **DOI:** 10.3390/medicina56110555
- **Type:** Research Article
- **Summary:** Innovative research proposing a fertility indicator equation (FIE) based on the rate of change in serum estradiol levels throughout the cycle. Demonstrates that daily estradiol serves as the best analyte for determining ovulatory cycle phase and fertile start day. Establishes that E2 levels ~411 pmol/L (112 pg/mL) indicate transition to fertile phase. Applicable to natural cycle monitoring in assisted reproductive treatment.

## Key Clinical Highlights

### Reference Ranges - Early Follicular Phase (Days 1-7)
- **Optimal:** 25-50 pg/mL (confirms adequate ovarian rest)
- **Normal:** 25-75 pg/mL (92-275 pmol/L)
- **Concerning:** >60-80 pg/mL (may indicate cyst or diminished reserve)
- **Critical Pre-IVF:** <30-40 pg/mL required to start stimulation

### Clinical Applications
1. **Fertility Assessment:** Day 3 testing with FSH for ovarian reserve
2. **IVF Screening:** Baseline assessment before ovarian stimulation
3. **Diagnostic Tool:** PCOS, premature ovarian insufficiency, hypogonadism
4. **Cycle Monitoring:** Natural cycle tracking in assisted reproduction

### Timing Importance
- Day 1 = first day of full menstrual flow (not spotting)
- Day 3 preferred for standardized fertility assessment
- No circadian variation (can collect any time of day)
- Irregular cycles require documentation and potentially multiple collections

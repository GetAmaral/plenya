### Conduct (1,665 chars)

Comprehensive clinical protocols:

**Physical Examination:**
- Bilateral scrotal inspection (asymmetry, erythema, edema, skin lesions)
- Bimanual comparative palpation (consistency, size, masses)
- Epididymis and spermatic cord assessment
- Varicocele search (standing position with Valsalva maneuver)
- Transillumination for hydrocele/spermatocele

**Ultrasound Indications:**
- Palpable mass
- Asymmetric testicular volume
- Persistent testicular pain
- Male infertility evaluation
- Scrotal trauma
- Suspected testicular torsion

**Ultrasound Protocols:**
- Color Doppler (vascular assessment for varicocele/torsion)
- Shear wave elastography (focal lesion characterization)
- Bilateral volumetric measurement (normal: 12-30mL)
- ESUR-SPIWG varicocele classification with Valsalva in standing position

**Tumor Markers:**
- AFP, beta-hCG, LDH before any surgical intervention

**Specific Management:**
- Solid intratesticular mass → urgent urology referral (radical inguinal orchiectomy)
- Grade III varicocele with subfertility → evaluate varicocelectomy
- Acute orchitis/epididymitis → culture-guided antibiotics (consider Chlamydia/Gonorrhea in sexually active young men)
- Voluminous hydrocele/spermatocele → consider surgical correction
- Long-term follow-up for patients with prior epididymoorchitis
- Patient education on warning signs

## Key Clinical Insights

1. **Testicular Cancer Epidemiology:**
   - Most common malignancy in men aged 15-34 years
   - 9,720 new cases annually (USA)
   - >95% cure rate with early detection
   - Strong association with prior epididymoorchitis (OR=38.24)

2. **Diagnostic Techniques:**
   - Multiparametric ultrasound superior to conventional ultrasound
   - CEUS and shear wave elastography improve diagnostic accuracy
   - May enable testis-sparing surgery instead of radical orchiectomy
   - Highly reproducible volumetric measurements

3. **Varicocele Assessment:**
   - Affects 15-20% of adult men
   - Can impact fertility and testicular function
   - ESUR-SPIWG standardized classification protocol
   - Grade III with subfertility may require varicocelectomy

4. **Clinical Controversy:**
   - USPSTF Grade D recommendation (2004, reaffirmed 2009, 2011) against testicular self-examination
   - Recent research challenges this recommendation
   - No formal USPSTF review in 12 years (normally 5-year cycles)
   - Male genital examination still recommended as part of routine physical

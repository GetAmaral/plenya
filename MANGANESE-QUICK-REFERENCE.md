## Key Clinical Points

### Essential Functions
- Cofactor for MnSOD, gluconeogenesis, urea cycle
- Bone formation, coagulation, immunity
- Narrow therapeutic window

### Toxicity Sources
- Occupational (welding, mining, batteries)
- Contaminated water
- Total parenteral nutrition
- Inhalation bypasses liver → direct CNS entry

### Neurotoxicity Mechanisms
- Oxidative stress
- Mitochondrial dysfunction (Complex I)
- Glutamatergic excitotoxicity
- Neuroinflammation
- Dopaminergic degeneration

### Clinical Presentation
- Parkinsonian symptoms (tremor, rigidity, bradykinesia)
- Cognitive changes
- Psychiatric symptoms (apathy, compulsivity, irritability)

### Diagnostics
- Serum Mn: 4-15 μg/L (reference)
- Brain MRI: T1 hyperintensity in basal ganglia
- 24h urinary Mn
- Liver function, iron panel

### Treatment
1. Remove exposure source
2. Chelation (EDTA) - limited efficacy in chronic cases
3. **Biotin 300mg/day** (2023 evidence)
4. Levodopa/carbidopa (variable response)
5. Iron supplementation if deficient

### Safety Threshold
**EFSA 2023:** 8 mg/day upper limit for adults

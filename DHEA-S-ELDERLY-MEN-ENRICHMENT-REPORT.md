## Clinical Content Summary

### Clinical Relevance Highlights
- DHEA-S declines 2-5% annually, reaching only 10-20% of young adult values by age 70-80
- Low DHEA-S predicts all-cause and cardiovascular mortality in elderly men (RR 1.41, 95% CI 1.18-1.69)
- Incident frailty inversely associated with baseline DHEA-S (OR 0.35)
- Association stronger in men <75 years and those with BMI <29 kg/m²
- Sex-specific effect: mortality association in elderly men but not postmenopausal women
- **Critical caveat:** Observational associations robust, but RCTs show no consistent benefits from supplementation

### Patient Explanation Highlights
- Explains DHEA-S as adrenal hormone declining naturally with age
- Links low levels to cardiac risk, physical frailty, premature mortality
- **Emphasizes:** Despite associations, supplementation studies showed NO significant benefits
- Addresses safety concerns (prostate issues)
- Advises against self-supplementation
- Explains physician will investigate treatable causes but likely won't prescribe replacement

### Clinical Conduct Highlights
- Reference ranges: typical 70+ range 20-200 μg/dL
- Investigate values <30 μg/dL in appropriate clinical context
- Assessment protocol: evaluate frailty markers, comorbidities, medications, adrenal function
- **Evidence-based recommendation:** DO NOT indicate routine DHEA supplementation (Level A evidence)
- Rare exceptions: research trials, documented adrenal insufficiency
- If considering off-label use: 25-50 mg/day, rigorous PSA monitoring q3-6mo
- **Preferred approach:** Focus on proven interventions (resistance exercise, protein nutrition 1.2-1.5 g/kg, vitamin D, CV optimization)
- View low DHEA-S as risk marker motivating intensification of proven preventive strategies, NOT therapeutic target

---

## Key Clinical Messages

1. **DHEA-S as Biomarker:** Strong predictor of frailty and mortality in elderly men, especially <75 years
2. **Supplementation Paradox:** Robust observational associations but no RCT benefits
3. **Safety Concerns:** Prostate cancer risk, BPH progression
4. **Clinical Action:** Use as risk stratification tool to intensify proven interventions, NOT as supplementation trigger
5. **Age/BMI Modifiers:** Stronger associations in younger elderly and non-obese individuals

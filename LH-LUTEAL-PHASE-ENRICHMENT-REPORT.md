**Result Interpretation:**
- Elevated LH (>15 mIU/mL): Primary ovarian insufficiency, PCOS, hyperprolactinemia

- Abnormal pulsatility patterns in LPD

**Clinical Correlation:**
- Comprehensive hormone panel: progesterone (>10 ng/mL target), estradiol, FSH, prolactin, TSH
- Detailed menstrual history with luteal phase calculation
- Ovulation confirmation via ultrasound or urinary LH monitoring
- Luteal phase <11 days considered abnormal

**Clinical Management:**
- Current evidence does NOT support progesterone supplementation in natural cycles
- Focus on optimizing follicular development and ovulatory quality
- Therapeutic options: clomiphene citrate, letrozole for follicular optimization
- Address hypothalamic factors: stress reduction, weight normalization, exercise adjustment
- Treat underlying conditions: hyperprolactinemia, thyroid dysfunction
- ART cycles: progesterone supplementation required due to endogenous LH suppression

**Follow-up:**
- Referral to reproductive endocrinologist for confirmed LPD with infertility
- Serial ultrasound follicular mapping
- Hormonal monitoring during treatment

## Key Clinical Messages

1. **LH is Essential:** Corpus luteum progesterone production is absolutely dependent on LH stimulation
2. **Reference Range:** 1.0-15.0 mIU/mL during luteal phase (significantly lower than ovulatory surge)
3. **Pulsatile Secretion:** LH is released in pulses; abnormal pulsatility characterizes luteal phase deficiency
4. **Luteal Phase Deficiency:** Defined as <11 days duration or mid-luteal progesterone <10 ng/mL
5. **Evidence-Based Treatment:** Focus on improving follicular quality, NOT progesterone supplementation in natural cycles
6. **Three Critical Roles:** Formation (luteinization), maintenance (progesterone production), pregnancy support (until 8-9 weeks)

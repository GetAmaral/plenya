**Testing Requirements:**

**Interpretation Guidelines:**
- Low SHBG (<13 nmol/L general, <17 nmol/L for insulin resistance prediction)
- Associated conditions: visceral obesity, insulin resistance, T2DM, metabolic syndrome, hepatic steatosis
- High SHBG (>71 nmol/L): aging, primary hypogonadism, hyperthyroidism, cirrhosis, anticonvulsants, estrogens

**Free Testosterone Calculation:**
- Gold standard: equilibrium dialysis
- Practical alternative: Vermeulen formula (total testosterone + SHBG + albumin)
- Diagnostic cutoff: <65 pg/mL with elevated SHBG and symptoms

**Additional Workup:**
- Low SHBG + metabolic syndrome: HOMA-IR, HbA1c, lipid panel, liver enzymes, abdominal ultrasound
- Testosterone therapy monitoring: SHBG every 6 months initially

**Clinical Considerations:**
- Acute variations: intense exercise (-10-15%), prolonged caloric restriction (+20-30%)

- Metformin consideration in diabetic men with SHBG <17 nmol/L
- Trial therapy justification: low-normal testosterone + elevated SHBG + symptoms in men >50
- Contraindications screening: PSA >4, hematocrit >54%, untreated severe apnea

## Key Clinical Insights

### 1. SHBG as Metabolic Biomarker
- Low SHBG (<17 nmol/L) independently predicts insulin resistance (p=0.029)
- Risk ratio of 0.10 for T2DM in highest vs lowest quartile
- Stronger association with metabolic syndrome than total testosterone alone

### 2. Age-Related Considerations
- SHBG increases ~1.3% annually after age 50
- Free testosterone declines faster (2-3%/year) than total testosterone (1.6%/year)
- Explains androgen deficiency symptoms despite normal total testosterone in older men

### 3. Obesity-SHBG-Insulin Resistance Axis
- Visceral obesity → hyperinsulinemia → suppressed hepatic SHBG production
- Creates vicious cycle: low SHBG → reduced bioavailable testosterone → worsened body composition → increased insulin resistance

### 4. Clinical Decision-Making
- SHBG mandatory when testosterone total is borderline or discordant with symptoms
- Free testosterone calculation (Vermeulen formula) essential for accurate diagnosis
- Combined interpretation: testosterone + SHBG + clinical presentation

### 5. Therapeutic Monitoring
- SHBG monitoring every 6 months during testosterone replacement therapy
- Normalization of androgens may secondarily increase SHBG
- Metformin consideration in diabetic men with low SHBG
